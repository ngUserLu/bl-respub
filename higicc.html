Experience:      The Hawaii Geographic Information Coordinating Council is a community organization that promotes GIS in Hawaii and provides its members with opportunities for interaction and cooperation. I was a member of HIGICC from September 2004 until June 2010 and was elected to consecutive two-year terms of service on the Executive Board. Board Members are responsible for organizing committees, execution of special projects, and the overall direction of the organization. I served on the committee for the 2006 PacGeo state-wide and regional Pacific conference, scheduled speakers for the Luncheon Series, and was the coordinator for GIS Day in 2006. I also led a committee that analyzed GIS and geospatial teaching in public and private K-12 schools and developed a GIS curriculumn offered to teachers to supplement their regular lessons.
